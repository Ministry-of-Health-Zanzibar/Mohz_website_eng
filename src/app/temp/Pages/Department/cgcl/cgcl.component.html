Chief Government Chemist Laboratory (CGCL)

One of the oldest laboratories in Zanzibar and East Africa, with over 100 years of history. Starting as a small unit, CGCL has grown to an Agency with forty-two dedicated staff.

History of CGCL

CGCL began as a small unit within a ministerial department and evolved into an Agency under the Laboratory Act No. 10 of 2011. It now provides quality laboratory services across multiple sectors in Zanzibar.

Objectives of CGCL

The general objective of CGCL is to provide quality, cost-effective laboratory services promoting health and environmental safety. Its core functions include:

  • Forensic Science Analysis: Analysis of forensic samples to aid investigations.
  • Chemical Analysis: Conducting chemical analysis on various substances.
  • Environmental Analysis: Assessing environmental samples to ensure public health safety.
  • Food and Drug Analysis: Ensuring the safety and quality of food and drug samples.

Facilities and Laboratories

CGCL operates in a renovated building housing three labs: Food, Chemical, and Forensic Science. Plans include extending facilities, renovating two more buildings, staff training, and procuring additional equipment.

Future Plans

Development plans focus on expanding facilities, increasing lab sections, and meeting the growing demand for reliable and effective laboratory services.
