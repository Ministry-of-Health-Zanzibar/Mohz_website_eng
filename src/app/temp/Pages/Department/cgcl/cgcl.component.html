Chief Government Chemist Laboratory (CGCL)

The Chief Government Chemist Laboratory (CGCL) is one of the oldest laboratories in Zanzibar and East Africa, with over 100 years of history. It started as a small unit and has grown to an Agency with forty-two staff.

History of CGCL

The CGCL began as a small unit within a ministerial department and has evolved into an Agency under the Laboratory Act No. 10 of 2011. Over the years, it has expanded its capabilities to provide quality laboratory services across various sectors in Zanzibar.

Objectives of CGCL

The general objective of CGCL is to provide quality and cost-effective laboratory services to all sectors to promote the health of the people and their environment. This is achieved through the following core functions:

  • Forensic Science Analysis: Analysis of forensic samples to aid in investigations.
  • Chemical Analysis: Conducting chemical analysis on various substances.
  • Environmental Analysis: Assessing environmental samples to ensure public health safety.
  • Food and Drug Analysis: Ensuring the safety and quality of food and drug samples.
  • Scientific Research: Conducting research on topics related to the core functions of CGCL.

Facilities and Laboratories

The laboratory currently operates in one renovated building, which houses three laboratories: Food, Chemical, and Forensic Science. There are plans to extend and renovate two more buildings, as well as to train staff and procure additional laboratory equipment.

Future Plans

The CGCL is undergoing a development plan that includes extending its facilities and increasing the number of laboratory sections. The aim is to ensure that the laboratory meets the growing demand for services and continues to provide reliable and effective laboratory results.
