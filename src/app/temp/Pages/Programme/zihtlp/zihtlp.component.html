Zanzibar Integrated HIV, TB, and Leprosy Program (ZIHTLP)

A vital initiative under the Department of Preventive Services and Health Education combining the AIDS Control and TB/Leprosy Control Programs to enhance healthcare service delivery.

HIV Status

First AIDS cases in Zanzibar were diagnosed in 1986. The epidemic is concentrated among sex workers (19%), drug users (11.3%), and MSM (2.6%).

Tuberculosis Status

Leprosy Status

Leprosy cases dropped from 177 in 2014 to 104 in 2015. High burden remains in South, Urban, and West districts.

Services Offered

  • HIV Testing and Counseling
  • PMTCT of HIV
  • STI and RTI Prevention
  • Care for PLHIV
  • Home Care Services
  • TB & Leprosy Control
  • Laboratory Services
  • Health Education
  • Information Strategies

Vision

To ensure equitable access to quality HIV, TB, and Leprosy services for all, especially vulnerable populations.

Mission

To make Zanzibar free from HIV, TB, and Leprosy, and eliminate stigma and discrimination.
