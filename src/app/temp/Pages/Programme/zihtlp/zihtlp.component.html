Zanzibar Integrated HIV, TB, and Leprosy Program (ZIHTLP)

The Zanzibar Integrated HIV, Tuberculosis, and Leprosy Program (ZIHTLP) is a crucial initiative under the Department of Preventive Services and Health Education, Ministry of Health Zanzibar. It combines the AIDS Control Program and the Tuberculosis and Leprosy Control Program to enhance service delivery and resource utilization.

HIV Status

The first cases of AIDS in Zanzibar were diagnosed in 1986, with the HIV epidemic initially being low. Zanzibar faces a concentrated HIV epidemic, predominantly among sex workers, drug users, and men who have sex with men, with rates of 19%, 11.3%, and 2.6%, respectively, as per the 2012 Bio-Behavioral study.

In 2016, an estimated 6,269 individuals (adults and children) were living with HIV, with 81% of them in the 15-49 age group.

Tuberculosis Status

Tuberculosis continues to be a major public health challenge in Zanzibar, with reported cases rising from 354 in 2005 to 855 in 2015. The 25-34-year age group is most affected, with men being more vulnerable than women.

Leprosy Status

The goal of leprosy control is to prevent disabilities through early detection and treatment. Zanzibar has seen a decrease in diagnosed leprosy cases, with only 104 cases reported in 2015, down from 177 in 2014. Zanzibar, however, still has high levels of leprosy in some districts, with South, Urban, and West districts showing the highest burden.

Services Offered

  • HIV Testing and Counseling Services
  • Prevention of Mother to Child Transmission (PMTCT) of HIV
  • Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs) and Reproductive Tract Infections (RTIs) Prevention Programs
  • PLHIV (People Living with HIV) Care and Treatment Services
  • Home Care Services
  • Tuberculosis and Leprosy Control Services
  • Laboratory Services
  • Information, Education, and Communication/Behavior Change Programs
  • Information Management Strategies

Vision

The vision of ZIHTLP is to provide technical guidance and collaborate with sectors and practitioners to ensure equitable access to quality HIV, Tuberculosis, and Leprosy services, especially to at-risk populations.

Mission

Zanzibar aims to be free of HIV, Tuberculosis, and Leprosy, ensuring that individuals infected or affected by these diseases are not stigmatized or discriminated against in the community.
