Zanzibar Integrated HIV, TB, and Leprosy Program (ZIHTLP)

A vital initiative under the Department of Preventive Services and Health Education combining the AIDS Control and TB/Leprosy Control Programs to enhance healthcare service delivery.

HIV Status

First AIDS cases in Zanzibar were diagnosed in 1986. The epidemic is concentrated among sex workers (19%), drug users (11.3%), and MSM (2.6%). In 2016, around 6,269 individuals were living with HIV, with 81% aged 15–49.

Tuberculosis Status

Tuberculosis remains a major challenge. Reported cases rose from 354 in 2005 to 855 in 2015, with the 25–34 age group and men most affected.

Leprosy Status

Leprosy control focuses on early detection and treatment. Cases dropped from 177 in 2014 to 104 in 2015, with South, Urban, and West districts still experiencing high burdens.

Services Offered

  • HIV Testing and Counseling
  • PMTCT of HIV
  • STI and RTI Prevention Programs
  • Care and Treatment for PLHIV
  • Home Care Services
  • TB and Leprosy Control
  • Laboratory Services
  • Health Education & Behavior Change
  • Information Management Strategies

Vision

To ensure equitable access to quality HIV, TB, and Leprosy services for all, especially vulnerable populations, through technical leadership and collaboration.

Mission

To make Zanzibar free from HIV, Tuberculosis, and Leprosy, and to eliminate stigma and discrimination against affected individuals.
