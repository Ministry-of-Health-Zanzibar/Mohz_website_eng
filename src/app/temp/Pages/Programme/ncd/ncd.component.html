Strategic Plan for Non-Communicable Disease (NCD)

The NCD Strategic Plan promotes awareness and builds a healthcare system for individuals with Non-Communicable Diseases. It emphasizes community education, infrastructure improvement, and disease management.

Strategic Goals

  • Promote NCD awareness in underserved areas and enhance policies.
  • Educate the public about NCD causes and risks.
  • Develop an inclusive NCD healthcare system.
  • Establish data collection and research systems.
  • Monitor and evaluate plan implementation.

Implemented Activities

  • Established Zanzibar Cancer Registry (147 cases recorded).
  • 12,212 women screened for cervical cancer (via Chinese doctors).
  • 371 women (3%) diagnosed and treated with electrical loop (LEEP).
  • 34 patients (0.28%) referred to Ocean Road Dar Hospital.

Community Awareness

Public education campaigns utilize radio and outreach programs to cover:

  • Risk factors, tobacco use, and poor nutrition.
  • Cancer screening and importance of early detection.
  • Prostate, cervical cancer, diabetes, and hypertension.
  • Connection between NCDs and nutrition.

Challenges

  • Limited hospital equipment for proper cancer data collection.
  • Incomplete patient data from wards and shehias.
  • Delays in processing and reporting cancer cases.
