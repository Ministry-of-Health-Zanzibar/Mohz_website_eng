Strategic Plan for Non-Communicable Disease (NCD)

The NCD Strategic Plan aims to promote awareness and create a health system that caters to individuals with Non-Communicable Diseases. The plan focuses on educating communities, improving healthcare infrastructure, and ensuring effective management of these diseases.

Strategic Goals

  • Increase promotion of Non-Communicable Diseases (NCD) in underserved areas to improve environmental policies.
  • Raise general awareness about the causes and risks of Non-Communicable Diseases.
  • Create a healthcare system that meets the needs of individuals with Non-Communicable Diseases.
  • Develop data collection systems and research findings.
  • Monitor and evaluate the implementation of the NCD Strategic Plan.

Implemented Activities

  • Establishment of a Cancer Registry in Zanzibar, with 147 cases in the database.
  • Collaboration with Chinese doctors to screen 12,212 women for cervical cancer.
  • 371 women (3%) diagnosed with early treatable symptoms through an electrical loop (Leep).
  • 34 clients (0.28%) diagnosed with advanced-stage cancer referred to Ocean Road Dar Hospital for further treatment.

Community Awareness

To raise public awareness on NCD prevention, its risks, early detection, and proper management, radio programs and community education are utilized. Topics covered include:

  • Risk factors of NCDs, tobacco control, and unhealthy nutrition.
  • Importance of cancer screening, blood pressure, diabetes, prostate cancer, cervical cancer.
  • Connection between NCDs and nutrition.

Challenges

  • Difficulty in collecting cancer information due to hospital equipment limitations in the cancer registry data.
  • Poor patient information collection from sources like wards and shehias.
  • Delays in cancer report processing.
