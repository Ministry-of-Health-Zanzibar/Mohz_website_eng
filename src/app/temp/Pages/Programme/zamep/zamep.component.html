Zanzibar Malaria Elimination Programme (ZAMEP)

The Zanzibar Malaria Elimination Programme (ZAMEP) plays a key role in the development, implementation, and evaluation of malaria control interventions, contributing to the achievement of Zanzibar's health goals and targets.

Strategic Alignment

ZAMEP is strategically aligned with the following national frameworks:

  • The National Strategy for Growth and Reduction of Poverty II (MKUZA II)
  • The Zanzibar National Health Policy (2009)
  • The Health Sector Strategic Plan II (2006/07-2010/11)

These alignments ensure that ZAMEP contributes to both national and international targets for malaria elimination.

Key Responsibilities

  • Development, implementation, and monitoring of malaria control interventions.
  • Evaluation of malaria control programs to assess effectiveness and impact.
  • Coordination with stakeholders at all levels of the health care delivery system.
  • Linking public and private organizations for quality assurance and health system strengthening (HSS).

Health System Strengthening (HSS)

ZAMEP facilitates health system strengthening by ensuring effective coordination, resource mobilization, and capacity building for malaria control, contributing to the overall improvement of public health in Zanzibar.
